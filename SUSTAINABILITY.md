# Sustainability & Funding Strategy

## Overview
This document outlines the long-term sustainability model 
for the BOSC Community Library.

## Funding Model Comparison

### Model 1: Red Hat Service-Based Model
Red Hat's model provides free software but charges for 
enterprise support, training, and professional services.

**For BOSC this means:**
- Library resources remain free and open to all
- Revenue generated from training workshops and certifications
- Government agencies pay for dedicated support contracts
- Community contributors are rewarded through employment

**Advantages:**
- Financially self-sustaining
- Encourages professional development
- Keeps core library free forever

**Disadvantages:**
- Requires large user base to generate revenue
- Risk of prioritizing paying clients over community needs

### Model 2: Foundation-Driven Model
A non-profit foundation like Mozilla or Wikimedia manages 
donations, grants, and sponsorships to fund operations.

**For BOSC this means:**
- Funding from government grants and NGOs
- Community donations through annual campaigns
- Corporate sponsorships from tech companies
- Independent governance board manages funds

**Advantages:**
- Community-first decision making
- No conflict between profit and public good
- Eligible for government and international grants

**Disadvantages:**
- Dependent on donations which can fluctuate
- Requires strong marketing for fundraising

## Recommended Model
A **hybrid approach** combining both models is recommended:
- Foundation governance for independence and grants
- Service contracts for government deployments
- Community donations for ongoing operations

## 🏛️ Government Proposal: Ministry of Education

**To:** Ministry of Education
**From:** BOSC Community Library Team
**Subject:** Adoption of BOSC Community Library Over Proprietary Alternatives

### Executive Summary
We formally propose that the Ministry of Education adopt 
the BOSC Community Library as the national standard for 
open educational resources.

### Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
Proprietary educational platforms charge per-user licensing 
fees that cost governments millions annually. The BOSC 
Community Library is completely free to use, modify, and 
distribute under the MIT License, reducing TCO to near zero.

| Cost Factor | Proprietary Solution | BOSC Library |
|---|---|---|
| Licensing Fee | $50,000/year | $0 |
| Customization | $20,000/year | $0 |
| Vendor Support | $15,000/year | Community-based |
| Total Annual Cost | $85,000/year | ~$5,000 (hosting only) |

### Vendor Lock-in Risk
Proprietary platforms create dependency on a single vendor. 
If the vendor raises prices or discontinues the product, 
the government loses access to all resources. BOSC Library 
eliminates this risk entirely — the government owns and 
controls its own copy forever.

### Recommendations
1. Adopt BOSC Library as the national open resource platform
2. Allocate annual budget for hosting and maintenance
3. Train teachers and students on using the platform
4. Contribute local curriculum resources back to the library

### Conclusion
The BOSC Community Library offers maximum educational value 
at minimum cost, with zero vendor lock-in and full 
transparency. We urge the Ministry of Education to lead 
Africa in open educational resource adoption.
